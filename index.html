Body Mass Index (BMI) is calculated by dividing weight in kilograms (kg) by height in meters (m) squared. Doctors often use BMI to determine if a person is underweight, normal, overweight, obese, or extremely obese. Obesity increases the risk of disease and health problems such as heart disease, diabetes, and high blood pressure. If you think you may be obese and concerned about weight-related health problems, you and your doctor can evaluate your health risks and discuss your weight loss options. Weight loss is usually possible through dietary changes, increased physical activity and lifestyle changes.
